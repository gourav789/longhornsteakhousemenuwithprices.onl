Why Prices May Differ

LongHorn Steakhouse prices can and do vary significantly for a number of reasons:

  • Geographic Location: A steak that costs $29.99 in a rural southern state may cost $38.99 or more in a major metropolitan area or high cost-of-living region. This is standard practice across all restaurant chains.
  • Franchise vs. Corporate Location: Individual franchise operators may set prices independently, leading to variations even within the same city.
  • Market Conditions: Beef prices, supply chain disruptions, and inflation can all cause menu prices to change, sometimes frequently and without public announcement.
  • Seasonal Changes: LongHorn regularly introduces seasonal menu items or limited-time offers at varying price points that may not be reflected on our site.
  • Promotion Periods: Happy hour discounts, loyalty rewards redemptions, or promotional pricing are not reflected in our standard price listings.
  • State and Local Taxes: Sales tax rates vary by state and municipality, which affects the total amount you pay at the restaurant. Our prices do not include taxes.

Our prices are meant to provide a general ballpark estimate to help you plan your meal and budget. They are NOT meant to be relied upon as guaranteed, fixed, or binding pricing. Before visiting a LongHorn Steakhouse, always check the current menu at longhornsteakhouse.com or call your local restaurant to confirm today's pricing.
