🔥 The LongHorn Steakhouse Story

LongHorn Steakhouse was founded in 1981 in Atlanta, Georgia by Chap Johnson. The first restaurant opened on Peachtree Road and quickly became known for its bold flavors, generous portions, and authentic Western steakhouse atmosphere. The name "LongHorn" pays tribute to the iconic Texas Longhorn cattle breed — a symbol of the American West and rugged frontier spirit.

The brand was acquired by Darden Restaurants in 2007, one of the largest full-service restaurant companies in the world. Under Darden's stewardship, LongHorn has grown from a regional Georgia chain to a nationwide phenomenon with 640+ locations across the United States, Puerto Rico, Guam, and even the Philippines.

What has never changed is LongHorn's commitment to its founding values: fire-grilled, boldly seasoned steaks made from USDA Choice beef, served in a warm, welcoming atmosphere that feels like a celebration every time. Their signature hardwood grill gives every steak that unmistakable smoky char that keeps diners coming back again and again.

From the iconic Outlaw Ribeye® — their biggest, boldest 20-oz bone-in cut — to the supremely tender Flo's Filet®, every steak is hand-selected, expertly seasoned, and cooked exactly to your preference. It's this unwavering dedication to quality that has made LongHorn one of America's most-loved casual steakhouse chains.
